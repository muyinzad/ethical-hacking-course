## 1.1 Introduction to Cybersecurity

### What is Cybersecurity?
Cybersecurity is the practice of protecting systems, networks, and programs from digital attacks. These attacks typically aim to access, change, or destroy sensitive information, extort money from users, or interrupt normal business processes.

### The Cybersecurity Landscape
```
Threat Actors:
├── Nation States (APT)
├── Cybercriminals
├── Hacktivists
├── Insider Threats
└── Script Kiddies

Attack Vectors:
├── Network
├── Application
├── Social Engineering
├── Physical
└── Mobile
```

## 1.2 Types of Hackers

### White Hat (Ethical Hackers)
- Authorized penetration testers
- Work with organizations
- Follow legal guidelines
- Disclose vulnerabilities responsibly

### Gray Hat Hackers
- No malicious intent
- May hack without authorization
- Often notify victims
- Legal gray area

### Black Hat Hackers
- Malicious intent
- Steal data, cause damage
- Financial gain
- Illegal activities

## 1.4 Ethical Hacking Methodology

### PTES (Penetration Testing Execution Standard)

```
1. Pre-Engagement Interactions
   └── Scope, legal, rules of engagement

2. Intelligence Gathering
   └── Reconnaissance, OSINT

3. Threat Modeling
   └── Identify targets, vulnerabilities

4. Vulnerability Analysis
   └── Scan, enumerate, identify

5. Exploitation
   └── Attempt to exploit

6. Post-Exploitation
   └── Maintain access, pivot

7. Reporting
   └── Document findings
```
